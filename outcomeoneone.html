1.7 - There are six main constraints to any project;

Risk - What can go wrong, and what can be done about it?
Resources - Who and what is required to do the work?
Quality - How close the outcomes matches the expectations?
Scope - What exactly is the expected outcome?
Time - When should the outcome be delivered by?
Cost - How much money is available to achieve this outcome?
These constraints are interlinked, affecting one will affect another. This means that the constraints must be balanced so that the quality does not suffer. For example, if time is not balanced properly then it will cost more to get things finished quickly, or if resources aren’t available then time will have to increase to allow them to become available, this may also cause cost to rise as to lesser impact the time needed. GDPR is the General Data Protection Regulation, and it’s main focus is to increase the protection for personal data. This means that a few things have changed since this has come into legal effect;
Active opt-in - Users must actively opt in to giving companies their data, and will be asked and have the option to decline.
Granular - Users should have as much detail as possible as to what their data is being used for, and have the option to opt out of certain uses.
Unbundling - Consent requests must now have their own section, they cannot be bundled in with accepting terms and conditions.
Not a precondition of service - Consent of data cannot be a precondition of signing up to a service unless necessary.
Named - It should be clear to the user who will be processing their data, including any third parties.
This means that users have access to better privacy over their data, choosing if and what to share with the site. This means that digital marketers will only be able to process the data they really need, as they will have to obtain legal justification as to why they are collecting the data. The maximum fine for ignoring GDPR is €20 million or 4% of your total worldwide annual turnover, whichever of those two amounts is the highest.
